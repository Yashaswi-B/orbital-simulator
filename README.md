# Orbital Simulator
### A Computational Model of Newtonian orbital mechanics
*Built independently alongside undergraduate course work at community college, 2026-2027*

---

## Why This Exists

I started this project with a specific goal in mind: to understand how the same mathematics I learn in the classroom can be used to build a visual representation of the path of a spacecraft travelling between planets.

I am an international student on a dependent visa. I have no work authorization and cannot take paid internships or formal research positions. This project is my answer to that constraint. If I cannot 
work inside an aerospace lab, I can build one. 

The simulator will be developed in four stages, each designed to match the concepts I am learning at the time. Stage 1 beings now, grounded in Newtonian gravity and basic numerical integration. My plan of each subsequent stage is to build on top of the coursework will be learning.

---

## Project Stages

### Stage 1 – 2D Newtonian Gravity and Euler Integration
*In progress – March 2026*

Implementing two-body Newtonian gravity with Euler method numerical integration and matplotlib visualization. I will verify results against Kepler’s Third Law as a physics consistency check.

### Stage 2 – RK4 Integration & Energy Conservation
*Planned Summer 2026*

### Stage 3 – 3D Orbital Mechanics & Mission Scenarios
*Planned Fall 2026*

### Stage 4 – Documentation & Polish
*Planned October 2026*
